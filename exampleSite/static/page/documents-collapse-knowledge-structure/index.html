claim

Document boundaries collapse knowledge structure

When a document is treated as the main unit of knowledge, many of the relationships that give meaning to the material become implicit, hidden, or lost.

A page, file, note, or database row can store information, but it does not automatically preserve why one idea supports another, where a quote came from, who disagreed, or what later changed. Those relationships usually live in a human reader's head or in conventions that a machine cannot reliably inspect.

That is why document-centric systems feel good for publication but weak for durable knowledge transfer. The information survives. The structure that made it useful often does not.
