claim

Copy and paste breaks lineage

When knowledge is reused by duplication instead of reference, provenance, attribution, surrounding context, and revision history become harder to preserve.

Ordinary reuse usually means making another anonymous copy. Once a quote, summary, screenshot, or excerpt is pasted into a new document, the connection back to the exact source version often becomes social rather than structural.

XanaNode treats that break as a core failure mode. Addressable fragments, typed relationships, and transclusion let a later projection show not only the reused passage, but where it came from, which version it came from, and how it is being used now.
