Non-Destructive Merge Review

A federation pass should surface overlap, compatibility, and candidate mappings without silently collapsing distinct local nodes.

XanaNode merge behavior is review-first. A federation pass can detect overlap, record possible matches, score compatibility, and show a structural diff without pretending two authors meant exactly the same thing. That is why merge reports, compatibility reports, and diffs are protocol artifacts in their own right: they let tools expose the overlap before any permanent collapse or absorption happens.
